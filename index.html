Fake News & Social Engineering Awareness

Understanding online manipulation and learning how to stay safe

About This Website

Fake news and social engineering scams manipulate people by exploiting emotions, trust, and lack of digital awareness. This website presents common examples of deceptive online practices and explains how users can recognize and avoid them.

Examples of Social Engineering Scams

1. Phishing Email (Fake Account Warning)

Description: An email claims your bank or email account will be suspended unless you verify your information immediately.

Why it works: It creates fear and urgency, pushing users to act without thinking.

How to spot it: Check sender addresses carefully and avoid clicking suspicious links.

2. Fake Online Giveaway

Description: A social media post claims you have won a free phone or gift card.

Why it works: It exploits excitement and the desire for rewards.

How to spot it: Legitimate companies do not randomly give prizes or ask for fees.

3. Fake News Headline

Description: A shocking headline claims a secret government or financial disaster.

Why it works: Emotional language causes panic and rapid sharing.

How to spot it: Verify information through trusted news outlets.

Common Social Engineering Tactics

  • Urgency: Pressuring users to act quickly
  • Authority: Pretending to be banks, governments, or companies
  • Fear: Threatening negative consequences
  • Reward: Offering prizes or benefits

How to Protect Yourself

  • Think critically before clicking links
  • Never share passwords or sensitive information
  • Use strong, unique passwords
  • Enable two-factor authentication
  • Verify information from multiple sources
